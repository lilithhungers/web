My Creative Ethos

I've been writing for a little while now – but what have I found out about my writing? What's important to me when I write, why do I even want to write at all?

There are a couple of reasons, the first and most important one being that I have a deep need to express my honest lived experience.

As a nuerodivergent gender non-conforming person I have lived for years being confused and afraid without understanding why.

Writing helps me cope with this, which leads to my second reason.
I write because if I didn't then I think I would break. When I write something it's like I pull off a squishy chunk from my brain, look at it in my hands, tie a balloon around it, then let it float alongside me, no longer weighing me down. Sometimes the balloons need to be filled again, but in general the catharsis of writing keeps me relatively okay.
